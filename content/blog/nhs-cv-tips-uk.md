Public sector recruitment in the UK — NHS, local councils, civil service, education, and housing associations — follows different rules from private companies. Understanding person specifications, values-based recruitment, and Trac Jobs portals helps you get past the first screen.

## How Public Sector CVs Differ

- **Person specification** — employers list essential and desirable criteria; your CV must address each
- **Values and behaviours** — NHS values (care, compassion, commitment) and civil service behaviours matter
- **Structured applications** — many roles use online forms with CV upload plus competency questions
- **Equality monitoring** — separate from your CV; do not include age, photo, or marital status on the CV itself

## NHS CV Structure

Recommended sections:

1. Personal details and professional registration (NMC, HCPC, GMC if applicable)
2. Personal statement aligned to band and specialty
3. Employment history — reverse chronological with month/year dates
4. Education and qualifications
5. Clinical or technical skills (role-dependent)
6. CPD and mandatory training (Manual Handling, Safeguarding, BLS)

### NHS Personal Statement Example

*"Band 5 registered nurse with 2 years acute medical ward experience. Proficient in patient assessment, medicines management, and multidisciplinary team working. Seeking Band 6 surgical ward role with commitment to compassionate, evidence-based care."*

## Addressing Person Specifications

When a job lists essential criteria:

| Criterion | Where to Address |
|-----------|------------------|
| "Minimum 6 months customer service" | Experience section with dates |
| "IT literate including MS Office" | Skills section + example in bullets |
| "Ability to work under pressure" | Achievement bullet with evidence |

Use the same wording as the person spec where honest. ATS and shortlisting panels keyword-match applications.

## Civil Service CV Tips

For Fast Stream and departmental roles:

- **Two pages maximum** for most grades
- Focus on **behaviours** — Seeing the Big Picture, Changing and Improving, Making Effective Decisions
- Use **STAR method** in supporting statements (Situation, Task, Action, Result)
- CV supports the statement — do not duplicate entire narrative

## Local Council and Housing Applications

Roles span admin, housing officers, social workers, and environmental services. Emphasise:

- Community impact and public service motivation
- GDPR and safeguarding awareness where relevant
- Experience with vulnerable clients or regulated environments

## ATS in Public Sector Portals

NHS Jobs (Trac), Civil Service Jobs, and council systems parse uploaded CVs. Avoid:

- Complex tables and multi-column layouts
- Headers/footers with contact info
- Scanned image PDFs — use text-based PDF export

## References and DBS

State *"References available on request"* — do not list referees on the CV. Mention current DBS level if relevant (Enhanced, Update Service) in qualifications or additional information.

## Non-Clinical NHS Roles

Reception, medical secretary, porter, and finance roles in NHS trusts follow standard professional CV rules with sector awareness:

- Patient confidentiality and GDPR
- Experience in high-volume, regulated environments
- Shift flexibility

See [receptionist CV template](/templates/receptionist-cv-uk) for front-of-house examples.

## Common Public Sector CV Mistakes

1. Ignoring the person specification — apply only if you meet essentials
2. Generic personal statements — mention trust, department, or service area
3. Missing registration numbers — NMC pin, GMC number where required
4. Informal tone — public sector expects professional language
5. Wrong document — uploading CV when form asks for supporting statement only

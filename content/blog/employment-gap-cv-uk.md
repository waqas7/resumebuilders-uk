An employment gap on your CV is not a career killer — but how you handle it matters. UK recruiters see gaps from redundancy, parental leave, illness, caring responsibilities, and travel. Honesty plus a focus on readiness beats hiding dates or padding with fiction.

## Should You Address Gaps on a CV?

**Yes, briefly.** A unexplained multi-year gap raises more questions than a one-line explanation. You do not need a full story on the CV — save details for the interview.

Options:
- One line in your personal statement
- A dated entry for the gap period (e.g. "Career break — family care, 2021–2023")
- Reframe as productive time if you upskilled or volunteered

## Employment Gap Examples on a UK CV

### Parental Leave
*"Career break — parental leave (2020–2022). Completed PRINCE2 Foundation and volunteer project coordination to maintain professional skills."*

See our [CV after career break example](/templates/cv-after-career-break-uk).

### Redundancy
List the role with end date. In personal statement: *"Operations coordinator returning to work after redundancy. Seven years' PMO experience; available immediately for hybrid roles in London."*

### Illness or Health Break
You are not required to disclose medical details. *"Career break for personal reasons (2022–2023). Now fully available and completed [certification/course] during recovery period."*

### Travel or Sabbatical
*"Sabbatical — extended travel and freelance consulting (2023–2024). Maintained client project work remotely for UK-based SME."*

## What Recruiters Want to See After a Gap

1. **Recent activity** — courses, volunteering, freelance, certifications
2. **Updated skills** — software, regulations, industry changes during your break
3. **Clear availability** — start date and work pattern (full-time, hybrid)
4. **Confidence** — positive framing without over-explaining

## Formatting Gaps Without Lying

Never extend previous job end dates or invent roles. Background checks and references will surface discrepancies.

Use month/year format consistently: *Jan 2019 – Mar 2021* then *Apr 2024 – Present* with a brief gap note or intervening activity entry.

## Functional vs Chronological CV After a Gap

UK employers prefer **reverse chronological** CVs. Pure functional CVs (skills-only, hidden dates) look like you are hiding something.

Better approach: chronological layout with a strong personal statement and skills section upfront. Lead with what you offer now.

## Cover Letter Strategy for Gaps

Use the cover letter to add two sentences on the gap — reason, what you did during it, and why you are ready now. Keep tone forward-looking.

## Returning After a Long Break

If your gap exceeds 3 years:
- Refresh LinkedIn and CV with current terminology
- Take a short course (CIPD, PRINCE2, Google certificates) to show commitment
- Consider temp or contract roles to rebuild recent experience line
- Network — many returners find roles through contacts, not cold applications

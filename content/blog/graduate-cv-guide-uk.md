Graduate recruitment in the UK is competitive — thousands apply to each scheme. Your CV must prove potential, not just a long work history. Recruiters look for degree class, internships, extracurricular impact, and clear motivation.

## Graduate CV Length and Format

- **One page** if you have limited experience (final-year students, recent grads)
- **Two pages** if you have placement year, multiple internships, or relevant part-time work
- Reverse chronological order throughout
- British English, no photo, no date of birth

See [best resume formats for UK jobs](/blog/best-resume-formats-uk-jobs) for full conventions.

## Essential Sections for a Graduate CV

### Personal Statement
Tailor per sector. Investment banking differs from charity work. Mention your degree, key skills, and target role in 3–4 lines.

Example: *"Finance graduate (First Class predicted) with spring internship experience at a Big Four firm. Strong Excel and financial modelling skills. Seeking analyst roles in London or Manchester."*

### Education (Prominent Placement)
List degree first with:
- University, course, dates
- Predicted or achieved classification
- Relevant modules (e.g. Corporate Finance, Data Analytics)
- A-Levels and GCSEs — summarise unless employer asks for full breakdown

### Work Experience and Internships
Include placement years, vacation schemes, and part-time jobs. Use STAR-style bullets: Situation, Task, Action, Result.

### Extra-Curricular and Leadership
Society committees, sports captains, volunteering, hackathons — especially if you lack internships. Treasurers and event organisers demonstrate commercial awareness.

### Skills
Technical (Python, SQL, Excel, CAD) and soft (presentation, teamwork). Match the job description.

## Graduate Scheme Applications

Large employers (Unilever, Civil Service, Big Four, banks) often use:

- Online application forms with CV upload
- ATS screening before human review
- Competency-based questions separate from the CV

Your uploaded CV must be ATS-safe. Use our [ATS-friendly template](/templates/ats-friendly-cv-template) and [ATS tips guide](/blog/top-ats-resume-tips).

## Placement Year on Your CV

Treat your placement like a full job:

- Job title (e.g. Marketing Assistant — Industrial Placement)
- Company name and dates (Sep 2024 – Aug 2025)
- 3–5 bullets with measurable outcomes

Placement experience often decides between similar graduates.

## No Internship? Still Competitive

Highlight:
- Dissertation or final project with real-world application
- Part-time retail or admin work (shows reliability)
- Volunteering with responsibility
- Online certifications (Google Digital Garage, HubSpot, LinkedIn Learning)

## International Graduates in the UK

If you studied in the UK on a visa, state work rights clearly if comfortable (e.g. "Eligible to work in the UK under Graduate Route visa"). See our [international student CV template](/templates/international-student-cv-uk).

## Common Graduate CV Mistakes

- Listing every module — pick 3–4 relevant ones
- Weak verbs — use "Analysed," "Presented," "Coordinated" not "Helped with"
- Unprofessional email — use firstname.lastname@ format
- One generic CV for every sector — tailor personal statement and skills

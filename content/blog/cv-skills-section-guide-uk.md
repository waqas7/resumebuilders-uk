The skills section is often scanned in under five seconds — by recruiters and by ATS software. A strong UK CV skills section is targeted, honest, and backed up by evidence elsewhere on the page.

## Where to Put Skills on a UK CV

Common placements:

1. **Below personal statement** — best for technical and role-specific skills
2. **Sidebar column** — popular in modern templates; ensure ATS can still parse (avoid graphics-only skill bars)
3. **Integrated into experience bullets** — skills demonstrated in context, plus a short dedicated list

## Hard Skills vs Soft Skills

### Hard Skills (Teachable, Measurable)
- Software: Excel, SAP, Salesforce, Adobe Creative Suite
- Tools: Forklift licence, POS systems, CRM platforms
- Methods: Agile, PRINCE2, financial modelling
- Languages: French (conversational), Mandarin (native)

### Soft Skills (Behavioural)
- Communication, leadership, problem-solving, time management
- Show these in experience bullets — a standalone list of "team player" without proof is weak

**Rule:** List hard skills explicitly. Demonstrate soft skills through achievements.

## How Many Skills to List

**8–15 relevant skills** for most roles. Quality over quantity — every skill should appear somewhere in your experience or education if questioned in interview.

Remove outdated tools (e.g. Windows XP, obsolete software) unless the job specifically requires them.

## Match Skills to the Job Description

1. Highlight 10–15 keywords in the posting
2. Include exact matches where truthful (e.g. "Stakeholder management" not just "Communication")
3. Mirror terminology — "MS Excel" vs "Advanced Excel" vs "Spreadsheets"

## Skills Section Formatting Mistakes

- **Skill bars and star ratings** — ATS cannot read graphics; use plain text lists
- **Irrelevant skills** — "Cooking" on a finance CV adds noise
- **Inflated claims** — "Expert Python" when you completed one online module
- **No evidence** — if you list "Project Management," include a bullet proving it

## Certifications and Licences

Group separately if you have several:

**Certifications:** PRINCE2 Foundation · CIPD Level 3 (in progress) · First Aid at Work

**Licences:** UK Full Driving Licence · FLT Counterbalance · CSCS Card

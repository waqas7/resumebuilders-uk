The UK care sector employs over 1.5 million people — and demand keeps growing. Care home jobs range from care assistants and support workers to senior carers and activities coordinators. Your CV is often the first filter before an interview, DBS check, and mandatory training.

## Types of Care Home Jobs in the UK

Common roles and what employers expect:

| Role | Typical Requirements |
|------|---------------------|
| **Care Assistant** | Personal care, moving & handling, compassion, shift flexibility |
| **Support Worker** | Similar to care assistant; may include community or learning disability settings |
| **Senior Care Assistant** | 2+ years experience, medication administration, mentoring new staff |
| **Activities Coordinator** | Engagement planning, creativity, dementia-friendly activities |
| **Kitchen / Domestic Assistant** | Food hygiene, infection control awareness |

Most entry-level care home jobs do not require a nursing degree — NVQ Level 2 in Health & Social Care (or willingness to work towards it) is common.

## What UK Care Home Recruiters Look For on a CV

Care home managers and agency recruiters scan for:

1. **Relevant care experience** — residential, domiciliary, or hospital support
2. **Safeguarding awareness** — adults at risk, reporting procedures
3. **Mandatory training** — moving & handling, first aid, infection control, medication (if applicable)
4. **DBS status** — Enhanced DBS for regulated activity; Update Service is a plus
5. **Shift flexibility** — weekends, nights, bank holidays
6. **Person-centred language** — dignity, wellbeing, individual care plans
7. **Reliability and teamwork** — handovers, documentation, multi-disciplinary working

They are not looking for fancy design. They want clarity, honesty, and evidence you can care safely.

## Essential Sections for a Care Home CV

### Personal Statement
3–4 lines combining compassion with practical skills:

*"Compassionate care assistant with 4 years in CQC-registered residential and dementia care homes. Skilled in personal care, moving & handling, and medication support under MAR chart protocols. Available for rotating shifts across Nottinghamshire."*

More examples in our [personal statement guide](/blog/cv-personal-statement-examples-uk).

### Skills Section
List plain-text skills ATS and recruiters keyword-match:

- Personal Care & Hygiene
- Moving & Handling
- Medication Administration
- Safeguarding Adults
- Dementia Care
- Infection Control
- First Aid at Work
- Care Plan Documentation

See our [CV skills section guide](/blog/cv-skills-section-guide-uk).

### Work Experience
Use achievement bullets with care-specific outcomes:

- ❌ "Helped residents with daily tasks"
- ✅ "Supported 20+ residents daily with dignity-focused personal care; maintained zero medication errors across 18 months under MAR chart supervision"

### Education and Qualifications
Include:
- NVQ/QCF Level 2 or 3 Health & Social Care
- Care Certificate (if completed)
- Enhanced DBS — state "Enhanced DBS (Update Service)" without certificate number on CV
- Mandatory training dates where relevant

## DBS Checks and Care Home Applications

Regulated care roles require an **Enhanced DBS check** with barred list checks. On your CV:

- State *"Enhanced DBS held (Update Service)"* if applicable
- Do not include DBS certificate numbers or full dates of birth
- If awaiting DBS, write *"DBS application in progress — available to start upon clearance"*

Employers often arrange DBS if you do not have one — showing awareness still helps.

## No Care Experience? How to Get Started

Many care workers enter from retail, hospitality, or volunteering:

- **Volunteer** at Age UK, charity shops with elderly clients, or hospital befriending
- **Complete the Care Certificate** or NVQ Level 2 — some employers fund training
- **Highlight transferable skills** — patience, reliability, teamwork, manual handling from warehouse or catering
- **Be honest** — "Healthcare support volunteer" beats inventing care home job titles

## Care Home CV Keywords for ATS

Online job boards (Reed, Indeed, CV-Library) and agency portals use ATS. Mirror language from each posting:

- Person-centred care
- Moving and handling
- MAR charts / medication administration
- Safeguarding adults
- CQC standards
- End-of-life care (if experienced)
- Dementia / Alzheimer's support
- Mental capacity and consent

## Care Home vs NHS CV

NHS roles often require professional registration (NMC, HCPC). Care home CVs focus on hands-on residential care, CQC context, and shift work — not clinical nursing qualifications unless you are applying as an RN.

For NHS-adjacent public sector advice, see our [NHS CV tips guide](/blog/nhs-cv-tips-uk).

## Common Care Home CV Mistakes

1. **Missing safeguarding mention** — employers need evidence you understand duty of care
2. **Vague compassion claims** — "caring person" without examples of personal care or documentation
3. **Omitting shift availability** — state weekend and night flexibility if true
4. **Wrong terminology** — use "care assistant" or "support worker" consistently with the job ad
5. **Graphics and skill bars** — break ATS; use plain lists
6. **Gaps unexplained** — brief note if you took time out; see [employment gap guide](/blog/employment-gap-cv-uk)

## Cover Letter for Care Home Jobs

Many care employers read cover letters — especially smaller homes and agencies. Explain **why care**, not just that you need a job. Two paragraphs on motivation, values, and one care achievement.

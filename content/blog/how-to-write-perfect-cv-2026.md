Writing a perfect CV in 2026 requires more than listing your jobs. Recruiters use applicant tracking systems (ATS) to filter candidates before a human ever reads your application. Here's how to stand out.

Whether you are applying through Indeed, LinkedIn, a company careers portal, or a recruitment agency in Manchester, Leeds, or London, the same principles apply: clear structure, evidence of impact, and formatting that both software and humans can read quickly.

## Start With the Right Format

Choose a clean, ATS-friendly layout. Avoid tables, text boxes, and graphics that parsers can't read. Our [ATS resume templates](/templates) are designed specifically for this.

Use a single-column or simple two-column format with clear section headings: Contact Information, Professional Summary, Experience, Education, and Skills.

For UK applications specifically, follow British conventions: use "CV" not "resume," write dates as Jan 2022 – Present, and stick to British English spelling throughout. Our guide to [best resume formats for UK jobs](/blog/best-resume-formats-uk-jobs) covers sector-specific layout choices in detail.

## Write a Strong Professional Summary

Your summary is the first thing recruiters scan. In 3–4 lines, state your role, years of experience, top skills, and what you bring to the employer.

Example: *"Marketing Manager with 8+ years driving digital campaigns. Expert in SEO, Google Analytics, and team leadership. Seeking to grow brand revenue at a scaling SaaS company."*

In the UK, this section is often called a personal statement or professional profile. It sits directly below your contact details and should be tailored for every application. For more examples by role, see our [CV personal statement examples for UK jobs](/blog/cv-personal-statement-examples-uk).

A strong summary answers three questions in under 80 words:

1. Who are you professionally?
2. What evidence do you have (years, tools, results)?
3. What type of role and sector are you targeting?

Avoid generic openers like "Dynamic professional seeking new challenges." Replace them with specifics: sector, seniority, and one measurable achievement.

## Quantify Your Achievements

Replace vague duties with measurable results:

- ❌ "Managed social media accounts"
- ✅ "Grew Instagram followers 150% in 12 months, generating 2,000+ leads"

Numbers prove impact. Use our [AI resume maker](/features#ai-resume-generator) to help craft achievement-focused bullet points.

UK recruiters respond well to metrics that reflect business outcomes: revenue saved, costs reduced, compliance rates, customer satisfaction scores, and team sizes managed. Even in non-commercial roles — NHS, local government, education — quantify where possible:

- "Supported 45 patients daily across a 28-bed ward, maintaining 100% medication administration accuracy"
- "Processed 200+ benefit applications per month with 98% first-time accuracy"
- "Reduced stock wastage by 18% through improved ordering and rotation procedures"

If you genuinely cannot attach a number, describe scope instead: "Managed end-to-end onboarding for all new starters across three regional offices."

## Optimise for ATS Keywords

Read the job description carefully. Mirror key terms naturally in your CV — job titles, tools, certifications, and industry jargon. Don't keyword-stuff; weave terms into your experience bullets.

Our [ATS optimisation checker](/features#ats-optimization) helps you spot missing keywords before you apply.

A practical UK keyword workflow:

1. Copy the job description into a separate document
2. Highlight repeated skills, qualifications, and responsibilities
3. Map each keyword to a specific bullet in your experience or skills section
4. Use the employer's exact phrasing where truthful (e.g. "stakeholder management" vs "working with people")

For a deep dive on parsing rules and file formats, read our [top ATS resume tips](/blog/top-ats-resume-tips). Common UK ATS platforms include Workday (used by Tesco, BT, and many FTSE firms), Taleo, and bespoke systems on NHS Jobs and Civil Service Fast Stream portals.

## Keep It Concise

For most roles, one to two pages is ideal. Every line should earn its place. Remove outdated roles, irrelevant hobbies, and filler phrases like "References available upon request."

UK length conventions are straightforward: one page for students and early-career candidates, two pages for most experienced professionals. Three pages is rare outside academia. Our [CV length guide for the UK](/blog/cv-length-guide-uk) explains when to cut older roles and how to prioritise content by relevance.

Cut ruthlessly:

- Jobs from more than 10–15 years ago (unless directly relevant)
- GCSE subject lists if you hold a degree
- Hobbies with no professional link
- Redundant soft skills ("communication," "teamwork") without proof

Every bullet should either demonstrate a skill the employer asked for or prove you can deliver results in a similar environment.

## Tailor Your CV for Every Application

The biggest difference between a good CV and a perfect one in 2026 is tailoring. Recruiters and ATS both reward relevance.

For each application:

- Adjust your personal statement to mirror the job title and sector
- Reorder skills so the most relevant appear first
- Move your strongest matching experience higher within each role's bullets
- Remove sections that do not support this specific application

## Structure Your Work Experience Section

UK employers expect reverse chronological order: most recent role first. For each position include:

- Job title
- Employer name and location (city is sufficient)
- Dates in month/year format (Mar 2021 – Present)
- 3–6 bullet points focused on achievements

**Example — Customer Service Advisor, British Telecom, Birmingham (Jun 2022 – Present)**

- Resolved 60+ inbound calls daily with 94% first-contact resolution, exceeding team target by 8%
- Trained four new starters on CRM workflows and complaint escalation procedures
- Identified recurring billing query pattern and suggested FAQ update, reducing repeat calls by 12%

Notice each bullet starts with a strong verb, includes scope or a metric, and connects to business value. Avoid passive phrases like "Duties included answering phones."

## Education and Qualifications on a UK CV

List education after experience unless you are a recent graduate with limited work history. Include:

- Degree title, university, graduation year, classification if 2:1 or above
- A-Levels or BTEC (subject and grade if strong; omit weak grades)
- GCSEs — list number of passes and Maths/English grades, or write "9 GCSEs including Maths and English"

Professional qualifications matter in many UK sectors: ACCA for finance, CIPD for HR, NMC registration for nursing, CSCS for construction. Place these in a dedicated "Qualifications" or "Professional Memberships" section if they are essential to the role.

## Skills Section Best Practice

Group skills logically: Technical, Software, Languages, Certifications. Match the job description's language.

- ❌ "IT skills, people skills, driving licence"
- ✅ "Microsoft 365, Salesforce, SQL basics | Full UK driving licence"

For sector-specific guidance, see our [CV skills section guide for the UK](/blog/cv-skills-section-guide-uk). Avoid skill bars, star ratings, and icon grids — ATS cannot read them and they add no value for human reviewers.

## Cover Letters and Supporting Documents

Many UK employers still expect a cover letter, especially in public sector and professional services. If the application asks for one, provide it — skipping it is a common reason for instant rejection.

Keep cover letters to one page. Structure: why this company, why this role, two proof points from your CV, confident close. Our [cover letter tips for the UK](/blog/cover-letter-tips-uk-2026) walk through the full process.

Some roles also request:

- Right to work confirmation (have your share code ready if applicable)
- DBS certificate details for regulated roles
- Portfolio links for creative or technical positions

Your CV should reference these where relevant without cluttering the layout.

## Export and Proofread

Before submitting, export as PDF and review on multiple devices. Typos and formatting glitches cost interviews.

Proofreading checklist:

- Read the CV aloud — awkward phrasing stands out
- Check every date for gaps or overlaps
- Verify phone number, email, and LinkedIn URL are clickable in the PDF
- Confirm British spelling: organise, centre, programme, licence (noun)
- Open the file on your phone to check layout does not break

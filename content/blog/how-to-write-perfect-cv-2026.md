Writing a perfect CV in 2026 requires more than listing your jobs. Recruiters use applicant tracking systems (ATS) to filter candidates before a human ever reads your application. Here's how to stand out.

## Start With the Right Format

Choose a clean, ATS-friendly layout. Avoid tables, text boxes, and graphics that parsers can't read. Our [ATS resume templates](/templates) are designed specifically for this.

Use a single-column or simple two-column format with clear section headings: Contact Information, Professional Summary, Experience, Education, and Skills.

## Write a Strong Professional Summary

Your summary is the first thing recruiters scan. In 3–4 lines, state your role, years of experience, top skills, and what you bring to the employer.

Example: *"Marketing Manager with 8+ years driving digital campaigns. Expert in SEO, Google Analytics, and team leadership. Seeking to grow brand revenue at a scaling SaaS company."*

## Quantify Your Achievements

Replace vague duties with measurable results:

- ❌ "Managed social media accounts"
- ✅ "Grew Instagram followers 150% in 12 months, generating 2,000+ leads"

Numbers prove impact. Use our [AI resume maker](/features#ai-resume-generator) to help craft achievement-focused bullet points.

## Optimize for ATS Keywords

Read the job description carefully. Mirror key terms naturally in your CV — job titles, tools, certifications, and industry jargon. Don't keyword-stuff; weave terms into your experience bullets.

Our [ATS optimization checker](/features#ats-optimization) helps you spot missing keywords before you apply.

## Keep It Concise

For most roles, one to two pages is ideal. Every line should earn its place. Remove outdated roles, irrelevant hobbies, and filler phrases like "References available upon request."

## Export and Proofread

Before submitting, export as PDF and review on multiple devices. Typos and formatting glitches cost interviews.

## Final Checklist

1. Clear contact info with LinkedIn URL
2. Tailored professional summary
3. Quantified experience bullets
4. Relevant skills section
5. ATS-friendly formatting
6. PDF export tested

Follow these steps and you'll have a CV that passes bots and impresses hiring managers in 2026.

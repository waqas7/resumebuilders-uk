Many UK job applications ask for a cover letter alongside your CV. Done well, it shows motivation, communication skills, and role-specific fit. Done poorly, it repeats your CV or reads like a generic template.

## Do UK Employers Still Read Cover Letters?

Yes — especially for competitive roles, graduate schemes, public sector jobs, and smaller employers who review applications manually. Online portals often have a separate upload field. Skipping it when required is an instant rejection.

## How Long Should a UK Cover Letter Be?

**One page maximum** — typically 250–400 words or three to four short paragraphs. Recruiters rarely read beyond that.

Use the same font and header style as your CV for a cohesive application pack.

## UK Cover Letter Structure

### Paragraph 1: Why This Role
State the job title, where you saw it, and one sentence on why you are interested. Mention the company by name.

### Paragraph 2: Why You
Pick 2–3 achievements from your CV that match the job description. Use numbers where possible.

### Paragraph 3: Why You Fit the Company
Show you researched the employer — values, recent news, sector challenges. Explain what you would contribute.

### Paragraph 4: Close
Confirm enthusiasm, state availability for interview, and thank them for considering your application.

## Cover Letter vs Personal Statement

Your CV personal statement is a snapshot. Your cover letter tells a short story — why this role, this company, now. Do not copy-paste your personal statement verbatim.

## Tone for UK Cover Letters

- Professional but human — avoid stiff Victorian formality
- Confident without arrogance
- British English spelling (organise, centre, programme)
- Active voice: "I led" not "I was responsible for being part of"

## Cover Letter Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Addressing "To Whom It May Concern"** — find the hiring manager or use "Dear Hiring Manager"
2. **Repeating your entire CV** — add context, not duplication
3. **Generic letters** — "I am a hard worker" without role-specific proof
4. **Wrong company name** — fatal copy-paste error
5. **Typos** — proofread twice; use spell-check set to UK English

## When to Skip a Cover Letter

Only skip if the application explicitly says cover letters are optional and you are tight on time — and even then, a strong letter can differentiate you. Never skip when the form marks it as required.
